The Growing Aviation Industry

The aviation industry is one of the world's fastest-growing industries, offering exciting careers, global opportunities, and job security. International standards set by ICAO ensure safety, innovation, and sustainable growth, making it ideal for ambitious students.

Aviation careers offer flexibility and job security. The industry operates under strict international standards set by organizations like ICAO (International Civil Aviation Organization), ensuring safety, innovation, and sustainable growth. Professionals in aviation enjoy working in a dynamic environment that combines technical expertise with the thrill of being part of a rapidly evolving industry.

Aviation is the only rapid worldwide transportation network, essential for global business. It generates economic growth, creates jobs, and facilitates international trade and tourism. The air transport industry supports 62.7 million jobs globally, making it one of the largest employment sectors. This includes direct employment in airlines, airports, and aircraft manufacturing, as well as indirect employment in related industries such as tourism, hospitality, and logistics.

ICAO (International Civil Aviation Organisation)

The International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO) was established to set standards for the safe and orderly development of international civil aviation. ICAO's methods include developing treaties, international standards, recommending best practices, and offering guidance to states.

ICAO's current objectives include: aviation safety, security, environmental protection, and sustainable development of air transport. The organization develops policies and standards that cover all aspects of civil aviation, including aircraft operations, airworthiness, air traffic management, airport infrastructure, and aviation security.

Concern over aviation security and terrorism has become increasingly significant since the September 11, 2001 attacks, though aviation security has been an international concern since ICAO's establishment in 1944. ICAO works closely with governments, industry stakeholders, and other international organizations to develop comprehensive security frameworks that protect passengers, crew, aircraft, and airport facilities from acts of unlawful interference.

Explore Aircraft Maintenance Engineering (AME)

Discover what it means to be an Aircraft Maintenance Engineer, the vital role they play in aviation safety, and the bright career opportunities that lie ahead.

Who is an Aircraft Maintenance Engineer (AME)?

An Aircraft Maintenance Engineer, also known as Licensed Aircraft Maintenance Engineer (LAME) is a licensed person who carries out & certifies aircraft maintenance nationally, internationally which is recognized by the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO).

What does an Aircraft Maintenance Engineer (AME) do?

An AME is a personnel who is responsible for the release of an aircraft or a component after maintenance. AME's ensures that at any time in its operating life, the aircraft complies with the airworthiness requirements in force and is in a condition for safe operation.

What is the future for Aircraft Maintenance Engineer (AME)?

The modern transport Aircraft today are highly sophisticated machines. Lots of different kinds of technologies are deployed in these advanced aircraft. The passenger and in-flight crew depend completely on the reliability of the aircraft parts and equipment. Thus, the job of aircraft maintenance engineers is crucial and in high demand.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about AME courses, admissions, and career opportunities

Aircraft Maintenance Engineering (AME) is a specialized field that involves the maintenance, repair, and certification of aircraft. An AME is a licensed professional authorized by DGCA (Directorate General of Civil Aviation) to carry out and certify aircraft maintenance. AMEs ensure that aircraft are airworthy and safe for operation, performing inspections, repairs, and maintenance on various aircraft systems including engines, airframes, and avionics.

To be eligible for AME course, candidates must have completed 10+2 (12th standard) with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) from a recognized board. The minimum aggregate marks required is usually 50% in PCM. Some institutes also accept candidates with a 3-year diploma in Mechanical, Electrical, or Electronics Engineering. Additionally, candidates must meet the age requirements (typically 16-28 years) and pass the medical examination as per DGCA standards.

The AME course duration is typically 3 years, divided into theoretical training (2.5 years) and practical training (6 months). After completing the course, students need to pass DGCA examinations to obtain the AME license. The total time to become a licensed AME can vary, but generally takes 3-4 years including the training period and obtaining the necessary experience.

AME licenses are categorized as follows:

  • Category A: Basic Aircraft Maintenance License
  • Category B1.1: Aeroplanes Turbine
  • Category B1.2: Aeroplanes Piston
  • Category B1.3: Helicopter Turbine
  • Category B1.4: Helicopter Piston
  • Category B2: Avionics

The fee structure for AME courses varies depending on the institute and location. Generally, the course fees range from 3-8 lakhs for the entire 3-year program. Government institutes typically charge lower fees (2-4 lakhs), while private DGCA-approved institutes may charge higher fees (5-10 lakhs). The fees usually include theoretical training, practical training, examination fees, and study materials. Many institutes also offer scholarships and education loan facilities.

After completing AME course and obtaining the license, graduates can work as Licensed Aircraft Maintenance Engineers in various sectors:

  • Airlines (Air India, IndiGo, SpiceJet, Vistara, etc.)
  • Maintenance, Repair, and Overhaul (MRO) organizations
  • Airports and ground handling services
  • Aircraft manufacturing companies
  • Aviation training institutes
  • Defense sector (Indian Air Force, Navy)
  • Private aviation companies
The starting salary typically ranges from 3-6 lakhs per annum, with experienced AMEs earning 8-15 lakhs or more.
